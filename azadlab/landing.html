The situation

Right now,
they know:

  • Where you are — right now
    To within a few metres. Updated continuously. Stored for years.
  • Who you talk to — and how often
    Metadata alone reveals your closest relationships, your health, your politics.
  • What you search for — going back years
    Health concerns, private fears, political questions — all logged and profiled.
  • What you believe — inferred and scored
    Political and psychological profiles built from clicks, pauses, and shares. Sold.
  • Your home — when you're there, when you're not
    Smart devices, phone pings, utility data — combined, analysed, monetised.

You didn't hide this. You didn't choose to share it.
You just used the internet.

When it gets worse

0 documented internet shutdowns · 2016 – 2024 · across 72 countries
November 2019 · Iran
The internet went dark for five days.
Protests were suppressed. At least 1,500 people were killed. The world found out afterwards.
February 2021 · Myanmar
Military coup. Internet cut.
18 months of rolling blackouts. Atrocities documented only through smuggled footage.
February 2022 · Ukraine
Infrastructure attacks began in the first hour of the invasion.
Satellite internet, telecoms, and power grids all targeted simultaneously.
2021 – 2023 · Ethiopia, Tigray
Two years without any telecommunications.
The longest documented communications blackout in history. Confirmed later from satellite images.
⚠ Right now: Iran has been offline for 40+ days.
This is not a news story. This is infrastructure as a weapon.

Why this exists

"In November 2019, I was in Tehran
when the internet went dark."

"I knew people were being killed outside.
The world didn't. Not for five days."

I had already built the tools to stay connected. A connection that worked without a reliable internet. Backups on hardware I controlled. Communication that didn't depend on any single server.

Not because I was an expert. Because I had prepared.

Arash Founder, Azad Lab Software engineer · Stockholm

The workshops

6 sessions.
Real tools.
No jargon.

Every session ends with something running on your own machine — not slides, not theory. A tool you own and control.

SESSION 01
Your Computer, Not Theirs
Move to Linux. Understand what you're running and why it matters. No experience needed.
SESSION 02
Your Photos & Media, Your Server
Stop storing memories in corporate clouds. Host your own photo library on hardware you own.
SESSION 03
Private Access from Anywhere
Connect to your own network securely from anywhere, without exposing it to the internet.
SESSION 04
Take Back Your Phone
Your phone is the most powerful surveillance device ever made. Learn to make it yours.
SESSION 05
Your Own Infrastructure
A small device at home can run your storage, backups, and automation — with no third party.
SESSION 06
When the Network Goes Dark
Power cuts. ISP outages. Infrastructure attacks. Offline tools, mesh communication, resilience.
