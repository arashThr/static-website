The real question

Do you own it,
or are you renting it?

  • Your photos and memories
    Stored on someone else's server. Subject to their pricing, their policies, their decisions. One change and your archive becomes inaccessible.
  • Your messages and conversations
    Run through apps that require an account and an active service. Someone else decides what you can say, who you can reach, and when the service stays on.
  • Your creative tools
    Rented monthly. Stop paying — lose access. The work you made is locked inside a format or a platform you don't control.
  • Your documents, notes, calendars
    Created by you. Hosted by a corporation. Available until it isn't — or until you've been logged out, flagged, or simply forgotten a password.
  • Your habits, history, and preferences
    The price of "free" services. Collected, profiled, and sold — not as a side effect, but as the entire business model.

There is a different way to use a computer.
You can own your tools instead of renting them.

When infrastructure fails

0 documented internet disruptions · 2016 – 2024 · across 72 countries
November 2019 · Iran
Five days. No internet. No way to reach the outside world.
People who had built their own tools — local storage, offline communication — kept functioning. Those who only rented access had nothing left.
February 2022 · Ukraine
Telecoms, power, and cloud services were targeted in the first hour of the invasion.
Resilience came from infrastructure that didn't depend on a single connection or a single provider.
2021 – 2023 · Ethiopia, Tigray
Two years without any telecommunications.
Information survived only where it was stored locally. The longest documented communications blackout in history.
Ongoing · Sweden, NATO, MSB
Sweden's civil preparedness strategy asks every household to be self-sufficient for 72 hours.
Food. Water. Power. The checklist exists. Digital preparedness is never on it — yet.
This is not about fear. It's about dependency.
What you own still works when everything else goes down.

The workshops

6 sessions.
Real tools.
Yours to keep.

Every session ends with something running on your own machine — something you own, control, and don't pay a subscription for.

SESSION 01
Your Computer, Not Theirs
Big Tech built beautiful cages. Your laptop and phone are products designed to harvest you, not serve you. We start by leaving.
  • Why Linux matters for digital sovereignty
  • Install Ubuntu or Fedora — your first free OS
  • The terminal: your new home base
  • Software without an app store controlling what you run
  • Filesystem layout and why it matters
You leave with:
A working Linux install. A terminal you're not afraid of. The mental model every other session builds on.
Ubuntu · Fedora · bash · apt/dnf
SESSION 02
Your Photos & Media, Your Server
Your memories don't belong in a corporation's datacentre. Build your own photo library and media server — Google Photos and Netflix, but yours.
  • Docker and containers — the concept, hands-on
  • Immich: private photo library with face recognition and mobile backup, all local
  • Jellyfin: your personal streaming server
  • Hardware options: old laptop, Raspberry Pi, VPS
  • Backup strategy: the 3-2-1 rule applied
You leave with:
Immich running with photos syncing from your phone. Jellyfin streaming your media. An understanding of how Docker actually works.
Docker · Immich · Jellyfin
SESSION 03
Private Access from Anywhere
You've built something worth accessing. Now connect to it privately, from anywhere, without opening your home to the internet.
  • Tailscale: private mesh network over WireGuard
  • A European VPS: cheap, reliable, yours
  • Caddy reverse proxy: automatic HTTPS, clean routing
  • DNS basics: pointing names at your own infrastructure
  • Access your photos and media from your phone, anywhere
You leave with:
A private network connecting all your devices. Your services accessible over HTTPS — no commercial VPN, no third party in the middle.
Tailscale · WireGuard · Caddy · Hetzner
SESSION 04
Take Back Your Phone
Your phone knows where you sleep, who you love, and what you fear. It reports all of it. This session is about taking that back.
  • What stock Android and iOS actually collect
  • GrapheneOS: hardened Android for Pixel devices
  • F-Droid: open-source app store, no Google account
  • Signal, Session, Matrix/Element for private messaging
  • AI on your own device — models that never leave your phone
You leave with:
A concrete plan for your device. Pixel owners: a flashed GrapheneOS install. Everyone: an audited, hardened phone.
GrapheneOS · F-Droid · Signal · llama.cpp
SESSION 05
Your Own Infrastructure
A small device at home can replace cloud storage, smart home hubs, and automation — with no subscription and no permission from anyone.
  • Raspberry Pi: from bare board to server
  • TrueNAS SCALE: professional NAS software, free — RAID, snapshots, file sharing
  • Home Assistant: open-source home automation, fully offline
  • Lights, climate, sensors, automations — without cloud dependencies
  • Local voice control — no Alexa, no Google
You leave with:
Home Assistant running your automations. A design for your home storage setup. No ongoing cost, no third-party account.
Raspberry Pi · TrueNAS · Home Assistant
SESSION 06
When the Network Goes Dark
Power cut. ISP outage. Infrastructure attack. What you built in sessions 1–5 still works — because you own it. This session makes it resilient.
  • Offline-first design: build for disconnection from the start
  • Encrypted backups with Borg and Restic
  • Syncthing: peer-to-peer sync with no servers involved
  • Meshtastic + LoRa radio: communication without internet
  • Your personal resilience checklist
You leave with:
A running encrypted backup solution. An understanding of offline and mesh tools. A concrete plan for when things go wrong.
Borg · Restic · Syncthing · Meshtastic
