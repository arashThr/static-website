SESSION 01

Linux as a foundation for digital freedom.
▼
  • Why Linux matters and which distro is right for you

  • No forced updates, no walled garden, no app store permission

A working Linux install that feels like home.
Ubuntu · Fedora · bash · apt/dnf
SESSION 02
Your Photos, Your Files, Your Server
Self-host your photo library. Own your memories.
▼
  • What platforms do with your photos (AI training, content analysis)
  • Account lockouts — no support exists for individuals
  • Docker + Immich: your private photo library, live by end of session
  • Tailscale: access it from your phone, privately, anywhere
You leave with:
Immich running and syncing from your phone.
Docker · Immich · Jellyfin · Tailscale · Caddy
SESSION 03
Your Own Little Server
A $50 device that blocks ads and automates your home.
▼
  • Raspberry Pi: first boot and what it can run
  • Pi-hole: network-wide ad blocking — covers every device at home
  • Home Assistant: offline home automation, no Amazon or Google
You leave with:
Pi-hole blocking ads on your whole network. Home Assistant controlling one thing.
Raspberry Pi · Pi-hole · Home Assistant
SESSION 04
AI: Use It, Own It
Run AI locally. Your questions stay on your machine.
▼
  • What LLMs actually are — demystified, in plain terms
  • Where your queries go when you send them to ChatGPT
  • Ollama: one command to run any open model locally
  • Chat, document Q&A, coding help — all offline
You leave with:
Ollama running with Gemma 4. A local AI assistant on your own machine.
Ollama · Gemma 4 · LM Studio
SESSION 05
When the Network Goes Dark
Communication tools that work without internet.
▼
  • BitChat: mesh messaging over Bluetooth/WiFi, no internet needed
  • Meshtastic + LoRa: long-range mesh without any infrastructure
  • Syncthing: peer-to-peer file sync, no servers
  • Lessons from Iran, Ukraine, Sudan — what actually worked offline
You leave with:
BitChat installed and tested. A personal offline resilience checklist.
BitChat · Meshtastic · LoRa · Syncthing

What's at stake

Your data.
Your call.

Your photos and memories
Used to train AI models. Your account can be locked with no human support. You're renting access to your own archive.
Your messages
Scanned for content, used to build profiles. End-to-end encryption is the exception, not the rule.
Your browsing and habits
Builds a detailed picture of who you are. Usually shapes ads. Sometimes something more consequential.
Your tools and creative work
Rented monthly. Stop paying and lose access. Locked into formats and platforms you don't control.
Your connectivity
Can be throttled, disrupted, or switched off entirely. What runs locally keeps working when it is.

The real threat

2026 · Iran — ongoing
80 million people cut off. Right now.
